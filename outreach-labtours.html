Tours

We open up the Electrical Engineering labs and take visiting groups through what studying engineering at NMU actually looks like — the equipment, the experiments, the people who use them every day.

What You'll See

Electronics & Circuit Lab

Hands-on workspace for circuit design, measurement, and testing. Home to oscilloscopes, signal generators, and PCB prototyping stations used in undergraduate practical work.

Electrical Engineering

Power Systems Lab

Equipment for high-voltage and power electronics experiments. Students work with transformers, motor drives, and protection relay systems that mirror real utility infrastructure.

Electrical Engineering

Control Systems Lab

PLC programming, feedback control systems, and automation experiments. Reflects the industrial side of electrical engineering and what students go on to work with in the field.

Electrical Engineering

Computer & Software Lab

Workstations used for simulation, MATLAB, CAD, and embedded systems programming. Where the theoretical design work gets turned into something that can be tested and verified.

The SWEAT Lab — Mapungubwe Building, Industrial Engineering

As part of extended campus tours, groups can also visit the SWEAT Lab — NMU's state-of-the-art Industrial Engineering facility housed in the Mapungubwe building. Equipped with advanced simulation software, sophisticated manufacturing equipment, and open collaborative workspaces, the SWEAT Lab shows what engineering looks like when it intersects with production, systems, and industry. It's a tangible demonstration of where the discipline goes beyond the classroom.

Book a Tour

Tours are open to high school groups, prospective students, and community organisations. We coordinate with the department to schedule visits around the academic calendar. Tours are guided by chapter members who are currently studying in these labs.

If you're a teacher, parent, or school coordinator looking to arrange a visit — or an NMU student who'd like to help run tours — reach out through the contact section on the main site.
