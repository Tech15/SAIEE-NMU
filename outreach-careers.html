The ECSA Professional Registration Path — at a glance

Most engineering graduates don't fully understand this process until they're already in industry. We cover it in detail at our sessions.

Step 1 · DegreeAccredited BEng or BEngTech from NMU or equivalent.
Step 2 · Register candidateRegister as Candidate Engineer with ECSA within 12 months of graduating.
Step 3 · LogbookDocument engineering experience under a registered mentor over 3 years minimum.
Step 4 · AssessmentSubmit logbook and pass ECSA competency assessment.
Step 5 · Pr EngFull Professional Engineer registration — legally able to sign off on engineering work.
