SAIEE Membership

How to Join
as a Student

Student membership is free, takes about 10 minutes to set up, and gives you a recognised professional credential before you even graduate. Here's exactly how to do it.

SAIEE (South African Institute of Electrical Engineers) is the professional body for electrical engineers in South Africa. Joining as a student costs nothing and starts building the professional record you'll need for ECSA registration later. Follow the steps below.

01

Go to the SAIEE membership portal

Open your browser and go to saiee.org.za. Click on Membership in the top navigation, then select Apply for Membership.

Takes 1 min
02

Select "Student" as your membership category

You'll be shown the membership tiers. Choose Student Member. This is for full-time students at an accredited institution — which NMU is. You do not need to pay anything.

Free
03

Fill in your personal and academic details

You'll need your full name, ID number, NMU student number, and your current year of study. Have your student card handy. Use your @mandela.ac.za email address where prompted.

Takes 5 min
04

Upload proof of enrolment

You'll need a proof of registration from NMU. Get this from the Student Portal (my.mandela.ac.za) — log in, go to Student Administration, and download your Proof of Registration for the current year as a PDF.

Takes 2 min
05

Submit your application

Review your details and submit. You'll receive a confirmation email. SAIEE processes student applications within a few business days. Once approved, you'll receive your member number.

Done
06

Tell us you've joined

Send your member number to the chapter so we can keep our records updated and make sure you're getting the most out of your membership. Contact us via the contact section on the main site.

Optional but encouraged

What you get as a student member

  • Official SAIEE member number and certificate
  • Access to SAIEE publications and events
  • Discounted rates on professional development events
  • Professional standing you can list on your CV
  • A head start on ECSA candidate registration after graduation

What happens after you graduate

When you graduate, your Student membership rolls over to Associate Member — the next tier. As you accumulate work experience under a registered engineer, you build your ECSA candidacy record. The chapter will host sessions on this process specifically for final-year students.
