Who We Are

The Chapter

We're the SAIEE Student Chapter at Nelson Mandela University — a student-run body inside the Department of Electrical Engineering. We exist to make sure NMU students aren't just graduates, but engineers with real industry awareness and a professional network before they even leave campus.

We run events, bring in industry contacts, and help you figure out where you fit in the field. Whether you're in first year or finishing your degree, this is where you start building your career.

Community & Schools

Outreach

We believe engineering starts long before university. Our outreach initiatives take the chapter beyond the campus — into schools, laboratories, and competitions — to inspire the next generation of electrical engineers.

High School Outreach

Chapter members visit local high schools to talk about electrical engineering as a career — what the degree involves, what the profession looks like, and how to get there. We go to schools that don't always get these conversations.

Active

NMU Laboratory Tours

We open the Electrical Engineering labs to prospective students and school visitors — a hands-on look at what studying at NMU actually means. While here, groups can also visit the SWEAT Lab in the Mapungubwe building, NMU's state-of-the-art Industrial Engineering facility equipped with advanced simulation software and manufacturing technology.

Active

Campus Career Engagement

We run sessions for current NMU students on ECSA registration, PR candidacy, and what life actually looks like after graduation — practical info that most lectures don't cover.

Active
Initiative · Active
First
Lego
League

NMU hosts First Lego League (FLL) — an international STEM competition for school teams. Students design, build, and programme Lego robots to solve real-world challenges, developing engineering thinking, teamwork, and problem-solving skills from a young age.

NMU has a dedicated FLL room on campus. The chapter supports the programme as part of our broader commitment to engineering education in the Eastern Cape — giving school teams direct access to a university engineering environment and the professionals who work in it.

SAIEE
Membership
Tiers

SAIEE has five membership categories. As a student you start with Student Membership — it's free, it counts, and it's your first step into the profession. The rest come as your career develops. Don't overthink it; just join.

01

Student

Full-time students at an accredited institution. Free.

02

Associate

Recent graduates entering the profession.

03

Full

Practising engineers with requisite experience.

04

Senior

Engineers of distinction and long practice.

05

Fellow

The highest honour. Awarded, not applied for.
