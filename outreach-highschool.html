What We Do
01

Career talks

Chapter members go to schools and talk to Grade 11 and 12 learners about electrical engineering — what you actually study, what jobs exist, and what the career looks like. No jargon, no recruitment pitch.

02

University application guidance

We walk learners through what NMU's engineering programme requires, how to apply, and what NSFAS and bursary options are available. A lot of capable students don't apply because nobody explained the process.

03

Q&A with current students

Learners get to ask us — actual engineering students — what university is really like. We answer honestly. The goal is informed decisions, not inflated expectations.

04

Focus on underserved schools

We specifically target schools that don't regularly receive this kind of engagement. Engineering as a career path is often invisible in communities where it could make the most difference.

About the Initiative

The High School Outreach programme started from a simple observation: many students who would make excellent engineers never consider engineering because no one in their immediate environment is an engineer. The gap isn't ability — it's visibility.

Chapter members volunteer their time to visit schools, particularly those in communities that are underrepresented in engineering at NMU. We're not there to sell the university. We're there to make sure capable learners know the path exists and what it actually involves.

If you're a school teacher or principal who'd like us to visit, or an NMU student who wants to volunteer for an outreach visit, get in touch via the contact section below.
